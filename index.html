Early Life and Education Struggle

  • Rajesh Khanna was born as Jatin Khanna to Lala Hiranand and Chandrani Khanna in a Punjabi family. His family later moved to Mumbai, where he completed his schooling at St. Sebastian's Goan High School. He continued his education at K.C. College, Mumbai, and pursued a degree in Arts.
  • Before entering the film industry, Rajesh Khanna worked as a ticket collector for the Indian Railways. However, his passion for acting led him to participate in All India Talent Contest organized by the United Producers and Filmfare magazine. He won the contest, which paved the way for his entry into the world of films.
  • Rajesh Khanna made his acting debut in 1966 with the film "Aakhri Khat." Although the film did not achieve great commercial success, his talent did not go unnoticed. It was in 1969 that he shot to stardom with the film "Aradhana," opposite Sharmila Tagore. The film's songs and his charismatic performance made him an overnight sensation.
  • Rajesh Khanna's career reached its zenith in the 1970s. He delivered a string of blockbusters, including "Anand," "Bawarchi," "Namak Haraam," "Safar," and "Amar Prem." His collaboration with director Shakti Samanta resulted in several successful films. "Anand" and "Bawarchi" showcased his versatility as an actor, while "Amar Prem" and "Namak Haraam" established him as the reigning romantic hero of the era
  • Rajesh Khanna's popularity was unparalleled. He earned the title "The Phenomenon" as he became the first actor in Bollywood to deliver 15 consecutive solo hit films between 1969 and 1971. His fan following was massive, with fans often lining up outside his bungalow, "Aashirwad," just to catch a glimpse of their beloved superstar.
  • Rajesh Khanna's exceptional acting skills earned him numerous awards and accolades, including three Filmfare Awards for Best Actor. He received the Padma Bhushan, India's third-highest civilian award, in 2005, in recognition of his significant contributions to Indian cinema
  • Tragically, Rajesh Khanna passed away on July 18, 2012, after a brief illness. His death marked the end of an era in Indian cinema, but his legacy lives on through his timeless films and the memories he created for millions of fans.
  • Rajesh Khanna's journey from a small-town boy to the "First Superstar of Indian Cinema" is a remarkable story of talent, perseverance, and charisma. His contributions to Indian cinema continue to be celebrated, and he will forever be remembered as a true legend of Bollywood.
